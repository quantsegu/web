Additional mortgage capacity from your extra payment

Standard amortizing loan math: if you can afford a fixed extra payment each period at a given rate and term, this shows the principal you could finance. Compare against a higher stress rate to see how affordability tests shrink borrowing power.

Additional loan capacity —

Payment-based capacity, not lender approval. Banks also limit mortgages by income, living expenses, other debt, stress-tested rates, loan-to-value (LTV), and property type. This tool answers the pure math question — “what principal fits this payment?” — not your guaranteed offer. Use the LTV and income notes on the left to record constraints from your lender conversation.

Principal vs interest over time
Formula (payment A, periodic rate i, payments n): P = A × (1 − (1+i)−n) / i when i > 0; if i = 0, P = A × n. Reverse: A = P × i / (1 − (1+i)−n).
