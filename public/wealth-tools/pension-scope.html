Replacement rate: how much of your salary will you keep?

Your replacement rate is projected pension income divided by your final pre-retirement salary. A rate of 70% or higher is often cited as a comfortable retirement target in OECD literature — though individual needs vary with housing costs, debt, and lifestyle.

Methodology & disclaimer

Projections are estimates based on 2025 tax and pension rules. Rules change — verify all figures with a licensed pension advisor in each jurisdiction. Exchange rates are approximate. This tool does not constitute financial advice. Replacement rates assume a full career from age 22 unless otherwise specified.

  • NL: AOW linked to Dutch minimum wage, adjusted twice yearly.
  • CH: BVG conversion rate may be reduced for above-mandatory capital.
  • DE: Subject to Nachhaltigkeitsfaktor demographic adjustment.
  • LU: CNAP contributions required; cross-border workers differ.
  • SG: CPF LIFE zero for non-citizens/non-PR; EP holders must self-fund.
  • AT: Reform ongoing; early retirement penalties before statutory age.
  • Post-retirement income: Day rates and tax estimates are illustrative; verify ZZP, flexi-job, and re-employment rules locally.
