Method & assumptions

All accumulation uses monthly compounding. Effective monthly return: r_m = (1 + r_annual − tax_drag)^(1/12) − 1. Contribution growth uses monthly rate g_m = (1 + g_annual)^(1/12) − 1.

Portfolio at retirement: FV = PV·(1+r_m)^n + PMT·[(1+r_m)^n − (1+g_m)^n] / (r_m − g_m) (if r_m = g_m, limit form is used).

Target income is entered in today's real terms. Nominal monthly need at retirement: target × (1 + inflation)^years. Capital required: nominal_annual_need / SWR. Sustainable income: portfolio × (SWR − tax_drag_on_withdrawal) / 12 where withdrawal tax drag equals annual tax/fees %.

Sequence-of-returns risk: equity-heavy portfolios may show higher long-term expected returns but larger drawdowns; realized paths can fall short of the deterministic projection. Bands on the growth chart show pessimistic (−2% return), base, and optimistic (+2% return) paths.
