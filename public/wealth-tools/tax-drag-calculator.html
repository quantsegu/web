How tax drag works

Gross return builds your portfolio. Each year, taxes skim dividends, wealth-based charges, and (if assumed) realized capital gains — shrinking the base that compounds forward.

Common sources of tax drag
Dividend withholding & income tax Cash flows out when distributions are taxed, even if you reinvest. High-yield markets amplify this drag.
Wealth tax / Box 3–style deemed return You may owe tax on portfolio value or fictional returns regardless of whether you sold anything — a direct hit to compounding base.
Annual capital gains realization Frequent trading or fund turnover triggers tax on gains each year. Deferred treatment lets more capital stay invested longer.
Country & wrapper choice The same gross return can produce very different net outcomes — pensions, ISAs, 2nd pillar, or relocating can change effective drag materially.
