Methodology & disclaimer

Net worth = liquid portfolio + pension capital (CH pillars 1–3a and NL AOW / supplementary / private pots) + property equity (value − mortgage). Liabilities are embedded in mortgage only.

Accumulation (pre-retirement): annual savings = gross compensation × savings rate + extra monthly investments × 12, grown with salary. Portfolio return is net of fee drag; dividend yield is taxed per jurisdiction. Pension pots grow at the pension return; CH pillar 2 receives salary-linked contributions; 3a and NL private receive fixed contributions until retirement.

Tax drag (simplified): Switzerland — income tax on salary, bonus, side income, and net rental; wealth tax on portfolio; dividend tax at income rate. Netherlands — box 1 on labour and rental; box 3 on portfolio using deemed return × effective rate (notional wealth tax). No double-tax treaties or cantonal/municipal detail.

Retirement income: CH — pillar 1 annuity + pillar 2/3a draw (4% capital rule on 2/3a); NL — AOW + supplementary + private annuity factors. Portfolio withdrawal uses your safe withdrawal rate on remaining liquid assets. Rental net of maintenance continues.

Scenarios: Switzerland and Netherlands apply respective rules throughout. Stay uses current country. Move later switches country at the move age. FX toggle converts EUR-denominated NL flows to CHF for display using your EUR/CHF rate.

This model is illustrative only — not tax, legal, or investment advice. Consult qualified advisers before relocating or restructuring wealth.
